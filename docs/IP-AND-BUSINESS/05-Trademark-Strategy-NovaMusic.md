## MARK-BY-MARK ANALYSIS

### Mark 1: NOVAMUSIC™

**Mark:** NOVAMUSIC™ (standard character mark)
**Short Forms:** Nova Music™, Nova™ (in music context)

**Trademark Assessment:**
- **Distinctiveness:** ARBITRARY — "Nova" (Latin for "new" or a type of stellar explosion) combined with "Music" creates an arbitrary compound mark. "Nova" has no established descriptive meaning in the music software industry. The compound is fanciful in the AI music platform context.
- **Conflicting Marks Search:** "Nova Music" searches return no registered trademark in Class 9 (software) or Class 42 (SaaS) from the USPTO database as of April 2026. Several "Nova" marks exist in unrelated categories. No "NovaMusic" single-word registration found in any relevant class.
- **Likelihood of Registration:** HIGH — The single-word compound "NOVAMUSIC" is highly distinctive and likely registrable.
- **Recommended Classes:**
  - Class 9: Computer software for music DJ performance, mixing, and AI setlist generation; downloadable mobile software applications for music
  - Class 38: Audio streaming services; real-time music broadcast services via internet
  - Class 41: Entertainment services in the nature of an online DJ and music mixing platform; live music event management services
  - Class 42: Software as a service for AI-powered music analysis and DJ automation

**Filing Priority:** IMMEDIATE (Priority 1)
**Estimated Filing Cost:** $350 × 4 classes = $1,400

**Brand Development Notes:** "NovaMusic" is the primary platform brand that should appear prominently in all product surfaces, marketing, app store listings, press materials, and investor communications. The single-word compound form provides maximum trademark strength. The name communicates newness ("Nova") and clarity of purpose ("Music") while the AI/galactic aesthetic creates a distinctive brand personality.

**Mark:** AI DJ MODE™ (standard character mark)
**Short Form:** AI DJ™

  - Class 41: Online DJ automation entertainment services powered by artificial intelligence

**Filing Priority:** IMMEDIATE (Priority 1)
**Estimated Filing Cost:** $350 × 2 classes = $700

**Importance:** "AI DJ Mode" is the beginner-facing product experience that will be the primary onboarding path for new users. This brand should be prominent in App Store/Google Play listings and social media marketing as it directly communicates the product's unique value proposition to non-DJ users.

### Mark 3: PARTY MODE™

**Mark:** PARTY MODE™ (standard character mark)

**Trademark Assessment:**
- **Distinctiveness:** SUGGESTIVE — "Party Mode" as a product experience name within a DJ platform is suggestive. While "party mode" is colloquially used, its application as a branded mobile-optimized DJ experience within NovaMusic creates distinctiveness.
- **Conflicting Marks Search:** "Party Mode" searches return several existing registrations including gaming and consumer electronics applications. Key registrations: PARTY MODE in Class 28 (games) and Class 9 (electronic games). The music platform context (Class 41, entertainment) may avoid direct conflict, but the Class 9 gaming mark warrants examination. A likelihood-of-confusion analysis is recommended.
- **Risk Assessment:** MEDIUM — Existing "Party Mode" registrations in gaming/electronics may create Section 2(d) likelihood-of-confusion refusals. Filing in Class 38/41 (streaming/entertainment) specifically for the music event context may avoid conflict with existing Class 9 gaming registrations.
- **Recommended Classes:**
  - Class 41: Online entertainment services in the nature of a mobile-optimized DJ and music event platform
  - Class 38: Real-time crowd engagement services for live music events

**Filing Priority:** IMMEDIATE (Priority 1) — with awareness of potential refusal and response strategy
**Estimated Filing Cost:** $350 × 2 classes = $700
**Action:** Conduct knockout search against existing "PARTY MODE" registrations before filing. If conflict identified, prepare evidence of different goods/services and channels of trade to support coexistence argument.

## ENFORCEMENT AND MONITORING STRATEGY

### Priority Enforcement Scenarios

1. **Platform Name Copying:** Any music platform using "Nova Music," "NovaMusic," or confusingly similar names in music software/services should be addressed with cease-and-desist.

2. **"AI DJ Mode" Copying:** Any platform marketing an "AI DJ Mode" feature without license should be addressed.

3. **"DJ Jeff" Persona Copying:** Any AI music platform creating a competing "DJ Jeff" AI persona should be addressed.

4. **"Crowd Pulse" Feature Copying:** Any platform using "Crowd Pulse" as a name for a crowd engagement scoring feature should be addressed.

5. **"Fire Zone" Feature Copying:** Any DJ platform using "Fire Zone" to name their track cue point detection feature should be addressed.

### Monitoring Program

- **Monthly:** USPTO trademark database search for new applications in Classes 9, 38, 41, 42 using keywords: "Nova Music," "AI DJ," "Crowd Pulse," "Fire Zone," "VibeMeter," "DJ Jeff"
- **Quarterly:** App Store and Google Play monitoring for competing app names
- **Semi-annually:** Comprehensive competitive landscape review of new music AI platforms for brand copying
